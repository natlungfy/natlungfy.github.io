How this was made

Using the New York Times Article Search API and web scraping, I managed to obtain 6503 NYT articles (=12,850,998 words) that mention the word "China" from January 1, 1995 to December 31, 2017. The breakdown is shown in the chart below.

After collecting all article text and organizing them by year, I cleaned the data by removing stop words and retaining only the nouns and adjectives in the articles. Then these were trained using the skip-gram model provided by the Gensim implementation of Word2Vec. From there I was able to get the most similar words.

After collecting the similar words, I mapped and visualized each word on a scatter plot with the help of t-SNE representations.

Due to copy right, the raw data of all article text will not be shared. However, you can find the data behind the main graphic, with all similar words sorted by year, here.
